## Foundation – German Basic Law 
<section style="padding:40px; background:#eef3fb; border-radius:12px; margin-top:20px;">



<p style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
<strong>German Basic Law, Article 12 GG – Occupational freedom</strong>
</p>

<p style="font-size:20px; margin-top:15px;">
“<em>All Germans shall have the right freely to choose their occupation or profession, their place of work and their place of training. Practice of an occupation or profession may be regulated by or pursuant to a law.</em>”
</p>

<p style="font-size:20px; margin-top:15px;">
This article forms the constitutional basis for the right to choose and access vocational education and training.
</p>

</section>


## Choice of occupations (illustrative slide) 
<section style="padding:40px; background:white; border-radius:12px; margin-top:20px;">

<h2 style="font-size:30px;">Occupational freedom – examples</h2>

<ul style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
  <li>Bank clerk?</li>
  <li>IT electronics technician!</li>
  <li>Cycle mechanic?</li>
  <li>Sales assistant???</li>
  <li>Motor vehicle mechatronics technician!</li>
</ul>

<p style="font-size:18px; margin-top:15px;">
The Basic Law guarantees the freedom to choose between different occupational pathways, including vocational training occupations.
</p>

</section>


## The dual system – two learning locations 
<section style="padding:40px; background:#f5f5f5; border-radius:12px; margin-top:20px;">

<h2 style="font-size:32px;">2. The dual system</h2>

<h3 style="font-size:24px; margin-top:10px;">Two learning locations – shared responsibilities</h3>

<div style="display:flex; flex-wrap:wrap; gap:30px; margin-top:20px;">

  <div style="flex:1; min-width:260px;">
    <h4 style="font-size:22px;">Company</h4>
    <p style="font-size:18px;">
      Practical, company-based training at the workplace.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div style="flex:1; min-width:260px;">
    <h4 style="font-size:22px;">Vocational school</h4>
    <p style="font-size:18px;">
      Theoretical and general education at a public vocational school.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

<p style="font-size:20px; margin-top:20px;">
<strong>Coordination between national laws and federal state laws ensures that both learning venues work together.</strong>
</p>

</section>


## Legal framework around the dual system 
<section style="padding:40px; background:white; border-radius:12px; margin-top:20px;">

<h2 style="font-size:30px;">Legal framework of the dual system</h2>

<div style="display:flex; flex-wrap:wrap; gap:30px; margin-top:10px;">

  <div style="flex:1; min-width:260px;">
    <h3 style="font-size:22px;">National laws</h3>
    <ul style="font-size:18px; line-height:1.6;">
      <li>Vocational Training Act (<strong>BBiG</strong>)</li>
      <li>Crafts and Trades Regulation Code (<strong>HwO</strong>)</li>
      <li>Training regulations</li>
      <li>Youth Employment Protection Act (<strong>JArbSchG</strong>)</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div style="flex:1; min-width:260px;">
    <h3 style="font-size:22px;">Federal state laws</h3>
    <ul style="font-size:18px; line-height:1.6;">
      <li>Compulsory Schooling Act</li>
      <li>Education laws of the federal states</li>
      <li>Skeleton curricula</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

</div>

<p style="font-size:20px; margin-top:20px;">
Approx. <strong>70%</strong> of training takes place in the company, and <strong>30%</strong> in the vocational school.
</p>

<p style="font-size:18px; margin-top:10px;">
<strong>German Basic Law, Article 12:</strong> Occupational freedom is the overarching principle.
</p>

</section>


##  Summary of the legal framework 

<section style="padding:40px; background:#eef3fb; border-radius:12px; margin-top:20px;">



<h3 style="font-size:24px; margin-top:10px;">National laws</h3>

<ul style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
  <li>Vocational Training Act (<strong>BBiG</strong>)</li>
  <li>Crafts and Trades Regulation Code (<strong>HwO</strong>)</li>
  <li>Youth Employment Protection Act (<strong>JArbSchG</strong>)</li>
  <li>Working Time Act (<strong>ArbZG</strong>)</li>
  <li>Collective Agreements Act (<strong>TVG</strong>)</li>
  <li>Federal Paid Leave Act (<strong>BUrlG</strong>)</li>
  <li>Law temporarily regulating the chambers of commerce and industry (<strong>IHKG</strong>)</li>
  <li>Federal Personnel Representation Act (<strong>BPersVG</strong>)</li>
  <li>Works Constitutions Act (<strong>BetrVG</strong>)</li>
  <li>Minimum Wage Act (<strong>MiLoG</strong>)</li>
</ul>

<h3 style="font-size:24px; margin-top:20px;">Federal state laws</h3>

<ul style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6%;">
  <li>General compulsory schooling</li>
  <li>Education laws of the federal states</li>
</ul>

<p style="font-size:20px; margin-top:20px;">
<strong>Coordination of both learning venues</strong> (company and school) takes place between the Federal Government and the federal states.
</p>

</section>


## Structure of the Vocational Training Act 
<section style="padding:40px; background:white; border-radius:12px; margin-top:20px;">

<h2 style="font-size:32px;">4. The German Vocational Training Act (BBiG)</h2>

<h3 style="font-size:24px; margin-top:10px;">Structure of the Act</h3>

<ul style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
  <li><strong>General regulations</strong></li>
  <li><strong>Vocational education and training contract</strong></li>
  <li><strong>Organisation of VET</strong></li>
  <li><strong>Research, planning, statistics</strong></li>
  <li><strong>The Federal Institute for Vocational Education and Training (BIBB)</strong></li>
  <li><strong>Regulations regarding fines</strong></li>
  <li><strong>Transitional and final provisions</strong></li>
</ul>

<p style="font-size:20px; margin-top:15px;">
The BBiG regulates the <strong>introduction and updating of training occupations</strong>.
</p>

</section>


## Regulations in federal law – basis 
<section style="padding:40px; background:#f5f5f5; border-radius:12px; margin-top:20px;">

<h2 style="font-size:32px;">5. Regulations in federal law</h2>

<h3 style="font-size:24px; margin-top:10px;">Basis</h3>

<ul style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
  <li>Determination of state-recognised occupations by the state.</li>
  <li>Stipulation of <strong>training regulations</strong>.</li>
  <li>Training regulations are used by the place of training as a template for a <strong>company training plan</strong>.</li>
</ul>

</section>


## Training regulations – core content 
<section style="padding:40px; background:white; border-radius:12px; margin-top:20px;">

<h2 style="font-size:30px;">Training regulations</h2>

<p style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
Training regulations:
</p>

<ul style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
  <li>Include the <strong>occupational title</strong>.</li>
  <li>Describe the <strong>occupation</strong>.</li>
  <li>Set out the <strong>skills, knowledge and competencies</strong> to be acquired and the necessary duration in binding form.</li>
  <li>Include the <strong>general training plan</strong>.</li>
  <li>Define the <strong>examination requirements</strong>.</li>
</ul>

<p style="font-size:20px; margin-top:15px%;">
They are implemented at company level via the <strong>training contract</strong> and the <strong>company training plan</strong>, forming a contractually regulated training arrangement.
</p>

</section>


## Core elements – training regulations 
<section style="padding:40px; background:#eef3fb; border-radius:12px; margin-top:20px;">

<h2 style="font-size:30px;">Core elements of the training regulations</h2>

<ul style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
  <li>Title of the training occupation.</li>
  <li>Duration of training: <strong>no shorter than 2 years</strong>, <strong>no longer than 3.5 years</strong>.</li>
  <li>Training profile: the occupational <strong>skills, knowledge and competencies</strong> that need to be imparted.</li>
  <li>General training plan: instructions regarding <strong>content and time structure</strong> for imparting skills, knowledge and competencies; includes the report book.</li>
  <li>Examination requirements.</li>
</ul>

</section>


##  Place of training and training staff 
<section style="padding:40px; background:white; border-radius:12px; margin-top:20px;">



<p style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
The place of training must offer:
</p>

<ul style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
  <li>Appropriate facilities (premises, machines, equipment etc.).</li>
  <li>An appropriate ratio between trainees/training places and skilled workers.</li>
</ul>

<p style="font-size:20px; margin-top:10px;">
Staff must demonstrably be in possession of:
</p>

<ul style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
  <li><strong>Personal and professional aptitude</strong>.</li>
  <li>Appropriate occupational and vocational teaching skills, knowledge and competencies (as per <strong>Ordinance on Trainer Aptitude, AEVO</strong>).</li>
</ul>

<p style="font-size:20px; margin-top:10px;">
The <strong>competent body</strong> (chamber of crafts and trades, chamber of commerce and industry or similar) monitors the suitability of the company and the trainers and can impose <strong>sanctions</strong> in the event of breaches.
</p>

</section>


## Training contract 
<section style="padding:40px; background:#f5f5f5; border-radius:12px; margin-top:20px;">

<h2 style="font-size:30px;">Training contract (company – trainee)</h2>

<p style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
The training contract is a particular form of contract of employment with additional provisions.
</p>

<ul style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
  <li>Registration via the chamber responsible → <strong>monitoring function</strong>.</li>
  <li>Stipulates:
    <ul>
      <li>Type, content, time structuring and aim of the VET (vocational qualification aspired to).</li>
      <li>Commencement, duration, regular daily training time (according to Youth Employment Protection Act, Working Time Act).</li>
      <li>Remuneration, probationary period, paid leave, prerequisites regarding notice etc.</li>
      <li>Rights and duties of both parties.</li>
    </ul>
  </li>
  <li>Written form → must be signed by both parties.</li>
  <li>No right to acceptance into a regular contract of employment → the contract expires when the examination is passed.</li>
</ul>

</section>


## Calculation of remuneration 
<section style="padding:40px; background:white; border-radius:12px; margin-top:20px;">



<ul style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
  <li>Annual increase in remuneration by year of training.</li>
  <li>Benefits in kind are possible (but no more than <strong>75% of gross pay</strong>).</li>
  <li>Allowance is paid <strong>monthly</strong>.</li>
  <li>Allowance is also paid if trainees are released to attend an inter-company training centre during training.</li>
  <li>Amount of remuneration is aligned to the <strong>collective wage agreement</strong> applicable in the industry or to a guidance value stipulated by the chamber (which may be undercut or exceeded).</li>
</ul>

<p style="font-size:20px; margin-top:15px;">
<strong>Minimum wage:</strong>
</p>

<ul style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
  <li>Does <strong>not</strong> apply to trainees.</li>
  <li>Does <strong>not</strong> apply to young people who have not completed a vocational qualification.</li>
</ul>

</section>


##  Examination system and competent bodies 
<section style="padding:40px; background:#eef3fb; border-radius:12px; margin-top:20px;">



<p style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
<strong>Competent bodies</strong> (e.g. chambers of industry and commerce – IHK, chambers of crafts – HWK and others) are responsible for:
</p>

<ul style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
  <li>Regulating company-based training.</li>
  <li>Counselling and advising companies and trainees.</li>
  <li>Monitoring training quality and suitability.</li>
  <li>Organising and conducting examinations via <strong>examination boards</strong>.</li>
</ul>

<p style="font-size:20px; margin-top:10px;">
The examination system comprises:
</p>

<ul style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
  <li>Company-based training.</li>
  <li>Intermediate examination.</li>
  <li>Final examination.</li>
</ul>

</section>


## Qualification and examinations 
<section style="padding:40px; background:white; border-radius:12px; margin-top:20px;">

<h2 style="font-size:30px;">Qualification – examinations</h2>

<ul style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
  <li>Final examinations in all recognised training occupations.</li>
  <li>Legal regulations exist in respect of:
    <ul>
      <li>Intermediate examination and final examination, or</li>
      <li>Extended final examination.</li>
    </ul>
  </li>
  <li>Prerequisites for admission include:
    <ul>
      <li>Written record of training.</li>
      <li>Participation in the intermediate examination.</li>
      <li>Exceptional regulations in special cases.</li>
    </ul>
  </li>
  <li>Object of examination: candidates are required to demonstrate <strong>employability skills</strong>.</li>
  <li>Execution: conducted by the examination board of the competent body.</li>
  <li>Final certificates:
    <ul>
      <li>Chamber certificate.</li>
      <li>Certificate from company.</li>
      <li>Vocational school certificate.</li>
    </ul>
  </li>
</ul>

</section>


## Crafts and Trades Regulation Code 
<section style="padding:40px; background:#f5f5f5; border-radius:12px; margin-top:20px;">

<h2 style="font-size:30px;">Crafts and Trades Regulation Code (HwO)</h2>

<ul style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
  <li>Law regulating the craft trades (<strong>HwO</strong> or <strong>HandwO</strong>).</li>
  <li>Second part – <strong>Vocational Education and Training</strong> (in this regard a special law accompanying the Vocational Training Act).</li>
  <li>Regulates:
    <ul>
      <li>Practising craft trades in the private sector.</li>
      <li>VET and continuing training in the craft trades.</li>
      <li>The master craftsman examination.</li>
      <li>Self-administration of this economic sector.</li>
    </ul>
  </li>
</ul>

</section>


## Youth Employment Protection Act 
<section style="padding:40px; background:white; border-radius:12px; margin-top:20px;">

<h2 style="font-size:30px;">Youth Employment Protection Act (JArbSchG)</h2>

<p style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
A law aimed at protecting young people (aged 15 to 17) who are working.
</p>

<p style="font-size:20px; margin-top:10px;">
With regard to young people, the law regulates the following:
</p>

<ul style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
  <li><strong>Number of working days per week:</strong> 5</li>
  <li><strong>Permitted hours of work:</strong> 6 am to 8 pm</li>
  <li><strong>Weekly working time:</strong> 40 hours</li>
  <li><strong>Flexibilisation solutions</strong> on individual days depending on the sector (extensions/shortenings)</li>
  <li><strong>Breaks:</strong> frequency and duration</li>
  <li><strong>Paid leave:</strong> 21–30 working days per year depending on age</li>
  <li><strong>Exceptions:</strong> weekend work (e.g. in hospitals)</li>
</ul>

</section>


## Compulsory Schooling Act – young people 
<section style="padding:40px; background:#eef3fb; border-radius:12px; margin-top:20px;">

<h2 style="font-size:30px;">6. Regulations in federal state law: Young people</h2>

<h3 style="font-size:24px; margin-top:10px;">Compulsory Schooling Act</h3>

<p style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
Mandatory school attendance:
</p>

<ul style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
  <li>Up to a certain age (maximum 18) or completion of schooling.</li>
  <li>Generally ten years of schooling.</li>
  <li>Requirement to register for a school, choose a school and attend school.</li>
</ul>

<h3 style="font-size:24px; margin-top:15px;">a) Compulsory full-time school attendance</h3>

<ul style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
  <li>Commences in general schooling and lasts for several years.</li>
</ul>

<h3 style="font-size:24px; margin-top:15px;">b) Compulsory vocational school attendance</h3>

<ul style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
  <li>Commences after expiry of the period of full-time compulsory schooling.</li>
  <li>Can be fulfilled via:
    <ul>
      <li>Attendance of lower and upper secondary schooling, or</li>
      <li>Within the scope of a programme of vocational education and training.</li>
    </ul>
  </li>
  <li>Concludes when a person:
    <ul>
      <li>Reaches the age of 18 (age of maturity), or</li>
      <li>Concludes VET, or</li>
      <li>Completes 12 years of school attendance.</li>
    </ul>
  </li>
</ul>

</section>


## Education laws of the federal states 
<section style="padding:40px; background:white; border-radius:12px; margin-top:20px;">

<h2 style="font-size:30px;">6. Regulations in federal state law: Schools</h2>

<h3 style="font-size:24px; margin-top:10px;">Education laws of the federal states</h3>

<p style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
Education laws stipulate the conditions under which teaching and learning take place and define the rights and duties of teachers and learners.
</p>

<h4 style="font-size:22px; margin-top:10px;">They stipulate:</h4>

<ul style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
  <li>Structure of the school system in the respective federal state.</li>
  <li>Teaching contents, compulsory schooling, school constitution, school providers, inspectorate, financing etc.</li>
</ul>

<h4 style="font-size:22px; margin-top:15px;">They govern:</h4>

<ul style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
  <li>Learning objectives and contents.</li>
  <li>Vocationally related subjects: <strong>two thirds</strong> of teaching.</li>
  <li>General education subjects: <strong>one third</strong> of teaching.</li>
  <li>Written and oral transcripts of records (relevant to final evaluation of trainees by the school).</li>
</ul>

<h4 style="font-size:22px; margin-top:15px;">Contain skeleton curricula on:</h4>

<ul style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
  <li>Compulsory schooling – full-time compulsory schooling / mandatory attendance at vocational school.</li>
  <li>Teaching staff: rights &amp; duties.</li>
  <li>Pupils: rights &amp; duties.</li>
  <li>Teaching: general aims and contents.</li>
  <li>Ratio between occupationally related and general subjects (2/3 to 1/3).</li>
  <li>Selection and scope of general subjects.</li>
  <li>Transcripts of records and certification.</li>
</ul>

</section>


## Minimum Wage Act
<section style="padding:40px; background:#f5f5f5; border-radius:12px; margin-top:20px;">

<h2 style="font-size:30px;">Minimum Wage Act (MiLoG)</h2>

<p style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
The Minimum Wage Act is a law to protect employees against low pay.
</p>

<ul style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
  <li>Applies everywhere in Germany since <strong>1 January 2015</strong>.</li>
  <li>Covers all employees and voluntary interns who have completed training from their 4th month in the company.</li>
  <li>The general minimum wage does not displace higher minimum wages applicable in a sector.</li>
</ul>

<p style="font-size:20px; margin-top:15px;">
<strong>Does not apply to:</strong>
</p>

<ul style="font-size:20px; line-height:1.6;">
  <li>Trainees, who conclude training contracts rather than contracts of employment.</li>
  <li>Young people who have not completed a vocational qualification.</li>
</ul>

</section>

# Legal Framework of VET in Germany - Quiz

This quiz is based on the GOVET presentation "Dual VET - Legal framework" and tests your understanding of the complex legal structure governing vocational education and training in Germany.

**Instructions:** Answer all 5 questions. Each question tests critical knowledge about the German VET legal system.

---

## Question 1: Primary Legislative Framework

Which federal law forms the primary legal basis for company-based vocational training in Germany and regulates vocational training preparation, VET in the dual system, advanced vocational training, and vocational retraining?

[[ ]] The Crafts and Trades Regulation Code (Handwerksordnung - HwO)
[[X]] The Vocational Training Act (Berufsbildungsgesetz - BBiG)
[[ ]] The Federal Education Act (Bundesbildungsgesetz)
[[ ]] The Employment Promotion Act (Arbeitsförderungsgesetz)
***
<section>

**Correct!** The Vocational Training Act (BBiG) is the cornerstone of German VET legislation. It:

- Regulates vocational training preparation, VET (dual system), advanced vocational training, and retraining
- Forms the legal basis for company-based training
- Entitles companies to conduct vocational education and training
- Applies to training in trade and industry, the public sector, and liberal professions
- Stipulates the prerequisites and conditions for training within companies

The Crafts and Trades Regulation Code (HwO) governs the craft trades sector separately, though its provisions largely equate to those of the BBiG.

**Source:** GOVET Legal Framework Presentation, BIBB Documentation

</section>

## Question 2: Federal Structure and Competencies

The German VET system operates within a federal structure where different aspects are regulated at different governmental levels. Which statement correctly describes this division of responsibilities?

[[ ]] Both company-based training and vocational school teaching are regulated by federal law
[[ ]] Both company-based training and vocational school teaching are regulated by federal state (Land) law
[[X]] Company-based training is regulated by federal law, while vocational school teaching is regulated by federal state (Länder) educational laws
[[ ]] The federal government regulates vocational schools, while states regulate company-based training
***
<section>

**Correct!** This reflects the dual nature of Germany's federal system in VET:

**Federal Level (Bund):**
- Regulates company-based training through the BBiG
- Develops training regulations for recognized training occupations
- Sets national standards for training content and qualifications

**Federal State Level (Länder):**
- Regulate vocational school teaching through their respective educational laws
- Design framework curricula for vocational schools
- Manage vocational school infrastructure and personnel

**Coordination Mechanism:**
Harmonization between federal and state levels occurs through various committees (e.g., Coordination Committee) to avoid conflicts caused by different laws. This ensures the two learning venues of the dual system work together effectively.

**Source:** GOVET Legal Framework Presentation, BIBB Documentation

</section>

## Question 3: Craft Trades Regulation

According to German law, what specific requirement exists for certain craft occupations listed in Annex A of the Crafts and Trades Regulation Code (Handwerksordnung)?

[[ ]] A university degree in engineering or a related field
[[ ]] At least 10 years of work experience in the occupation
[[X]] A master craftsperson's examination (Meisterprüfung) or comparable qualification for self-employment
[[ ]] Mandatory registration with the Chamber of Commerce and Industry
***
<section>

**Correct!** The Crafts and Trades Regulation Code has special provisions:

**Key Points:**
- Membership in the skilled craft sector in Germany is **not** determined by company size or turnover, but **by law**
- The Crafts and Trades Code (Handwerksordnung) determines which occupations belong to the crafts sector
- **Annex A** lists all occupations for which a master craftsperson's examination is a prerequisite for self-employment
- The legislator demands this qualification for occupations that are:
  - Particularly hazardous, and/or
  - Require special training for safety and quality reasons

**Rationale:**
This requirement ensures:
- High quality standards in craft trades
- Safety for consumers and workers
- Preservation of specialized knowledge and skills
- Protection of the "Meister" (master craftsperson) title

**Examples of Annex A occupations:** Electrical installations, plumbing, automotive mechanics, masonry, etc.

**Source:** GOVET Legal Framework Presentation, BIBB Documentation

</section>

## Question 4: Training Regulations and Standards

Training regulations (Ausbildungsordnungen) are essential instruments in the German VET system. Which of the following statements about training regulations is most accurate?

[[ ]] Training regulations are voluntary guidelines that companies can choose to follow
[[ ]] Each company develops its own training regulations based on industry needs
[[X]] Each recognized training occupation has legally binding training regulations that set out the training content in detail
[[ ]] Training regulations are developed exclusively by the Federal Ministry of Education
***
<section>

**Correct!** Training regulations are crucial legal instruments in the German VET system:

**Key Characteristics:**
- Every recognized training occupation has **legally binding** training regulations
- They set out the **content of training in detail**, including:
  - Occupational profile and competencies to be acquired
  - Structure and duration of training
  - Examination requirements
  - Minimum requirements for the training

**Development Process (Consensus Principle):**
Training regulations are developed through a **consensual process** involving:
- Federal government representatives
- Federal state (Länder) representatives  
- Employer organizations
- Trade unions/employee representatives
- Vocational education experts

**Legal Status:**
- Enacted by the responsible federal ministry (usually the Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs and Climate Action)
- Binding for all training companies offering that occupation
- Ensure **standardized quality** and **national recognition** of qualifications

**Coordination with Schools:**
Framework curricula for vocational schools are developed in parallel to ensure alignment between workplace and school-based learning.

Currently, Germany has **327 recognized dual vocational training occupations** (as of 2025).

**Source:** GOVET Legal Framework Presentation, BIBB Documentation

</section>

## Question 5: Oversight and Quality Assurance

The German VET system includes various mechanisms for oversight and quality control. Which institutions are primarily responsible for monitoring and supervising company-based training to ensure compliance with legal requirements?

[[ ]] The Federal Ministry of Education and Research (BMBF) directly supervises all training companies
[[ ]] Vocational schools are responsible for monitoring companies in their region
[[X]] The competent bodies (zuständige Stellen), primarily the Chambers (e.g., Chambers of Commerce and Industry, Chambers of Crafts), supervise and advise training companies
[[ ]] Local employment agencies oversee training quality and compliance
***
<section>

**Correct!** The chamber system is fundamental to quality assurance in German VET:

**Competent Bodies (Zuständige Stellen):**
The BBiG designates specific bodies as responsible for supervising vocational training:

**Main Chambers:**
- **Chambers of Commerce and Industry (IHK):** For commercial and industrial occupations
- **Chambers of Crafts (HWK):** For craft trades occupations  
- **Agricultural Chambers:** For agricultural occupations
- **Chambers of Liberal Professions:** For professions like lawyers, doctors, architects

**Key Responsibilities:**
1. **Registration and Approval:**
   - Register training contracts
   - Approve training companies and trainers
   - Maintain registers of training relationships

2. **Monitoring and Supervision:**
   - Monitor compliance with training regulations
   - Conduct workplace inspections
   - Verify trainer qualifications (AEVO)

3. **Advisory Services:**
   - Counsel companies on training matters
   - Support trainers and trainees
   - Mediate in conflicts

4. **Examination Administration:**
   - Organize and conduct intermediate and final examinations
   - Establish examination boards with employer and employee representatives
   - Issue certificates

**Legal Basis:**
These responsibilities are anchored in the BBiG and HwO, making chambers statutory bodies with public law responsibilities.

**Dual Oversight:**
While chambers supervise company-based training, **vocational schools** are supervised by state educational authorities, reflecting Germany's federal structure.

**Source:** GOVET Legal Framework Presentation, BIBB Documentation

</section>

## Quiz Summary

Congratulations on completing the quiz on the Legal Framework of VET in Germany!

**Key Takeaways:**

1. The **BBiG** and **HwO** form the legal foundation of Germany's dual VET system
2. Germany's **federal structure** creates a division between federal regulation of company training and state regulation of vocational schools
3. **Master craftsperson qualifications** are legally required for self-employment in certain craft occupations
4. **Training regulations** ensure standardized, nationally recognized qualifications through a consensus-based development process
5. **Chambers** play a central role in supervising training quality and administering examinations
